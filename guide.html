A note from Stavros

Most mythology content cites Wikipedia or popular retellings. I don't. Every claim traces back to the oldest available source — because that's where the story is actually different from what you've been told. This is what I have open when I write. Now you can follow along.

What's in the reading list
  • I
    The Iliad & Odyssey
    Homer
    The foundation. Which books to read first. The passages every Krios episode traces back to — including the ones that contradict the popular version.
    8th century BC · Perseus Digital Library (free)
  • II
    The Histories
    Herodotus
    The first historian. He records myth alongside fact — knowing which is which changes everything. Essential for Thermopylae and the Persian Wars episodes.
    5th century BC · Books 7–9 for Greek history
  • III
    Odes
    Pindar
    The most important non-Homeric source. Pindar contradicts Homer on key myth details — and he's often older. This is where the Achilles and Perseus episodes diverge from what you expect.
    5th century BC · Nemean, Pythian, Isthmian odes
  • IV
    Description of Greece
    Pausanias
    The ancient tourist who walked every sacred site and wrote down what he found. Essential for the S2 tourist-anchored episodes — he visited Cape Tainaron, the Archilocheion, and more.
    2nd century AD · 10 books, all available online
  • V
    Naturalis Historia
    Pliny the Elder
    The ancient world's encyclopedia. Where marble comes from. Who carved it. Why Parian marble was the most valuable stone in the ancient world. The backbone of EP009.
    1st century AD · Book 36 for art and stone
  • VI
    Library of Mythology
    Pseudo-Apollodorus
    The systematic mythology compendium. Where to find the version of a myth nobody talks about. Perseus, Theseus, the genealogies — all here in the clearest ancient form.
    1st–2nd century AD · The go-to reference
  • VII
    Parallel Lives
    Plutarch
    The ancient biographer. He synthesizes all the earlier sources and names them — invaluable for tracing contradictions back to their origin.
    1st–2nd century AD · Life of Theseus, Life of Themistocles
  • VIII
    Theogony & Works and Days
    Hesiod
    The cosmogony. Where the gods come from. How the Greek world understood time and creation before Homer. The foundation every other source builds on.
    8th century BC · Older than most of Homer
