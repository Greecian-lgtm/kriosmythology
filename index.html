μῆνις
mênis

Not ordinary anger. A divinely-sanctioned, cosmically consequential wrath — the kind that reshapes the course of history and implicates the gods themselves.

Homer opens the entire Iliad with this single word. Not battle, not glory, not Troy. Mênis. Every subsequent death in the poem flows from this one word in the first line. No other Greek hero gets mênis. It belongs to Achilles alone.

The popular reading is that Achilles sulks. The primary source reading is that he exercises the only power available to a man whose purpose has been publicly destroyed: withdrawal. With the full weight of divine endorsement.
