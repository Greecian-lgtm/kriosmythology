The Word

Homer opens the Iliad with menis

Not battle, not glory, not Troy. Menis. Every death in the poem flows from that first line. No other mortal in Homer gets menis — it belongs to gods and to Achilles alone, and that tells you everything about what Homer thought of him.

The Reading

Not sulking — orchestrating

The popular reading is that Achilles sulks. The primary source reading is that he exercises the only power available to a man whose purpose has been publicly destroyed: strategic withdrawal, with divine endorsement behind it.

The Connection

EP001 — Achilles  ·  EP006 — Troy

Both episodes trace the same thread: what Homer actually wrote versus what the popular retelling has become over two millennia of retelling.

The Method

Primary sources. Always oldest first.

Cite the oldest source, not the most famous. Flag contradictions between sources. Never trust a retelling without tracing it back to the text. That is the Stavros Commentary rule — applied to every episode, every claim.
