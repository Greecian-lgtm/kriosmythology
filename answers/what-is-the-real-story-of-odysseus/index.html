What Is the Real Story of Odysseus?

By Stavros Krios · Primary sources: Homer Odyssey, Homer Iliad, Sophocles

Direct Answer

Odysseus is the king of Ithaca and hero of Homer's Odyssey — a 24-book epic describing his 10-year journey home from Troy. The Odyssey opens not at the beginning of his journey, but in year ten, when he is trapped on Calypso's island — a structural choice by Homer that places the backstory in flashback. He is also a major figure in the Iliad, where his cunning is as important as his fighting ability.

The popular version of Odysseus as a simple adventure hero misses the complexity Homer built into the character. Odysseus lies, manipulates, and survives through intelligence rather than strength. The ancient Greeks debated whether he was admirable or morally questionable — Sophocles portrayed him as a cynical schemer, while Homer's Odyssey treats him with genuine sympathy.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long did Odysseus's journey take?

The Trojan War lasted 10 years, and Odysseus took another 10 years to return home — 20 years total away from Ithaca. His wife Penelope waited the entire time, fending off suitors who assumed Odysseus was dead.

Was Odysseus a hero or a villain?

He was a hero — but a morally complex one. Homer treats him sympathetically. Sophocles' Ajax presents him as ruthless. Virgil's Aeneid, written from a Trojan perspective, portrays him as treacherous. His reputation depended entirely on whose side was telling the story.

What primary source describes Odysseus?

Homer's Odyssey, composed in the 8th century BC, is the primary source. The Iliad also features Odysseus prominently. Sophocles' Ajax presents a darker version. Virgil's Aeneid gives the Trojan perspective.
