Greek Mythology — What the Sources Actually Say

Every answer cites the oldest primary source, not the most famous retelling. This is what Homer, Hesiod, Apollodorus, and Pausanias actually wrote.

  • Was Medusa always a monster — Apollodorus, Bibliotheca
  • Did Pandora open a box or a jar — Hesiod, Works and Days
  • Was Hades evil — Homer, Iliad 9.158-159
  • Did the Trojan War really happen — Thucydides 1.9-11
  • Who were the Cycladic people — Renfrew, Keros excavations
  • What is the Portara on Naxos — Archaeological record, 6th c BCE
  • Did Athena punish Medusa — Apollodorus vs Ovid comparison
  • What does kleos mean in Greek — Homer, Iliad 9.413
  • Why is Delos sacred in Greek mythology — Homeric Hymn to Apollo
  • Did Zeus fear Nyx — Homer, Iliad 14.259 — misattributed
  • Were the Titans evil — Hesiod, Theogony
  • What happened at Cape Tainaron — Pausanias, Description of Greece
  • What were the Eleusinian Mysteries — Homeric Hymn to Demeter
  • Did Theseus abandon Ariadne — Plutarch, Life of Theseus
  • What is xenia in Greek culture — Homer, Odyssey Book 9
  • Where was Apollo born — Homeric Hymn to Delian Apollo
  • What is the meaning of ate in Greek — Homer, Iliad 19.91-94
  • Who built the Lion Terrace on Delos — 7th century BCE, Naxian marble
  • What is leitourgia in ancient Greece — Aristotle, Athenian Constitution
